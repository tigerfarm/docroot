Keep It Simple

"Everything should be made as simple as possible, but no simpler."
A quote often attributed to Albert Einstein.
See the Quote Investigator for further details.

Einstein's actual quote,
   "It can scarcely be denied that the supreme goal of all theory is to make the irreducible basic elements as simple and as few as possible without having to surrender the adequate representation of a single datum of experience."

Or, simply restated,
   "Systems should be complete and simple as possible."

Which has the feature of Occam's razor,
   When presented with competing hypotheses that make the same predictions, one should select the solution with the fewest assumptions.

Lao-Tzu, the author of the Tao Te Ching, named simplicity, as one of his top three virtues.
   "I have just three things to teach: simplicity, compassion, and modesty."
